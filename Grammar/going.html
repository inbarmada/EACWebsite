Double Verbs & Going to
Many useful Spanish phrases contain two verbs. In these cases, only the first verb should be conjugated to the noun. To write the full verb phrase, simply write the first conjugated verb, then, if needed, a preposition (such as "a"), followed by the second verb in its infinitive form.

One especially useful example of this is for saying things set in the future. To do this, you would simply use the verb "ir", followed by whatever you want to say. This analogous to saying "I am going to ____" in English.

Here are some examples:
Yo voy a viajar en London. Ella quiere comprar una casa nueva.
Los chicos van a jugar en la playa. Tú debes limpiar pronto.
Ellos quieren ayudar a sus madres. Nosotros vamos a correr a la escuela.
Él necesita pagar para su comida. El tren puede llegar muy tarde.
