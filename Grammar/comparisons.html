Comparisons
We all compare stuff. It's human nature. And since we already do this in English, we might as well do it in Spanish as well. In fact, comparing things in Spanish in much easier than it seems. However, comparing inequalities and equalities do have some different grammar rules.

To say that something is more/less than something else, all you need to do is use the phrase: "más/menos" ______ "que." This is quite similar to the English structure of "more/less" ____ "than." In between these two words, you can compare just about anything, including nouns, adverbs, and adjectives.

Here are some examples:
Él tiene mucho más mascotas que yo tengo.
Su teléfono es más caro que mio.
Pienso que él corre solo un poco menos rápido que Usain Bolt.
Tengo menos lapízes que mi amiga.


To say that something is the same as something elseis a little harder. For adjectives, you use "tan" ______ "como." This is quite similar to the English structure of "as" ____ "as." For nouns, you would have to change the "tan" to "tanto/tanta/tantos/tantas" according to the noun. For verbs, you begin with the verb and then add "tanto como"; if the verbs are compared with an adverb, put it in between the "tan" and "como".

It's not as hard as it sounds! Here are some examples:
Él tiene tantos mascotas como yo tengo.
Su teléfono es tan barato como mio.
Pienso que él corre tan rápido como Usain Bolt.
Tengo tantas rosas como mi amiga.
Yo canto tanto bien como Beyonce.
