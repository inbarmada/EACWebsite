Superlatives
We can humans can have strong opinions sometimes. We not only know who is better, but we also know who is best. Declaring that something is "the most" out of a group is an example of using relative superlatives.

The structure of relative superlatives is somewhat similar to the of comparisons. However, you must put a definite article ("el" or "la") in front of the "más/menos," and follow the adjective with "de," rather than "que," as well as specify what group is being compared.

The examples will doubtlessly clarify this topic:
Él es el chico más gordo de nuestra escuela.
Ella es la chica menos inteligente de la clase.
Usain Bolt es el hombre más rápido del mundo.
Yo soy el joven menos interesante de Tejas.
