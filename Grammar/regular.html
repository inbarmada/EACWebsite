Regular Verbs
The regular conjugation is used to talk in the present tense. Depending on the subject, the infinitive form must be changed to fit the sentence. Moreover, each of the three types of inifinitive verbs (-ar, -er, and -ir verbs) is conjugated slightly differently, although the structure itself is quite similar. The following is a table showing how the ending of the verb changes according to its conjugation.
Subject -ar -er -ir
Yo -o -o -o
Tú -as -es -es
Él/Ella -a -e -e
Ellos/Ellas -an -en -en
Nosotros -amos -emos -imos


Here's an example with real verbs:
Subject Hablar Comer Vivir
Yo Hablo Como Vivo
Tú Hablas Comes Vives
Él/Ella Habla Come Vive
Ellos/Ellas Hablan Comen Viven
Nosotros Hablamos Comemos Vivimos
